History

Dulce Nombre de Maria is a district located in the Department of Chalatenango. It was established in 1790 by several spanish families who were sent by Baron Carandelet, general captain of Guatemala's kingdom. It received the title of village on April 21,1910, until later when it became the official district on July 15,1919. Before forming a part in the department of Chalatenango, Dulce Nombre De Maria was part of the San Salvador department since June 12, 1824 to May 22, 1835, and the Cuscatlan department from May 22, 1835 to February 14, 1855. Dulce Nombre de Maria has been part of Chalatenango since February 14, 1855. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dulce_Nombre_de_Mar√≠a) Image: Sign of Dulce Nombre de Maria. Transport to City/ Ways to Get Around the City

Getting to the City

Getting to the city is very easy no matter where you come from. Small and big cars alike can get to the city with ease. The roads are well paved and the view adds up to a nice driving experience. If you are coming from The capital, get into La troncal del Norte. Pass by the 4a Brigada de Infanteria El Paraiso Chalatenango. Then, turn left onto Calle a Dulce Nombre de Maria to arrive at your destination(https://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&q=como+llegar+a+dulce+nombre+de+maria&ie=UTF-8&ei=eonBUJPbIseJiAKP4IHYDQ&ved=0CAUQ_AUoAA)

Getting Around the City

Now that you have arrived, you will be able to enjoy the many things that Dulce Nombre de Maria has to offer. El Pital Highland specializes in ecological tourism, and for all nature-loving guests, there are different hikes with different levels of difficulty accompanied by specialized guides. They also offer other nature activities such as: bird observation, biking, or just simply enjoy the wonderful views from highest place in El Salvador. Although it is located in a high place, access to it is easy and you can use a normal car to get there. Prices range from 29 to 58 dollars a person, making it a really affordable place. If you have never been to this place, it is highly recommended to come and spend a weekend close to the sky in a fresh climate all around clouds. Dulce Nombre de Maria Church

Located in the heart of the town, this church is the place where most Catholics around town meet on Sundays at 10:00 A.M. Initially, the church belonged to Santo Tomas Tejutla, another subsidiary of Chalatenango. Later in 1842, it passed to be an independent community. In the year 1942, the church was redesigned and it is said that it had a colonial type feel which was maintained until 1990. The building had to be reconstructed in 1983 and resisted hits of armed conflict. Since 2010, the church is still in constant remodeling from initiative of the Italian priest Renzo Ferrazzo.( http://www.elsalvador.travel/iglesia-dulce-nombre-de-maria/; translated by page maker) Image: Dulce Nombre de Maria Church. Outdoor Activities

El Manzano Ecological Park

Located 10 minutes away from the heart of the city, El Manzano is a place with great historical memory, flora, and fauna. Besides all of its natural beauty the park also is part of great history, since it was a bastion of resistance and a zone under control of salvadorean rebel forces during the conflicts of the 1980's. Within the park, there is a museum with documents, photographies, and equipment used during the conflict. This park formed a part of the mission of the united nations who helped contribute in the process of a peace pact which put end to the the armed conflict in January 16, 1992. With 260 acres full of many types of trees, 127 species of birds, 67 species of orchids, and more than 120 plants with medical properties, make for one of the richest ecological experiences in El Salvador. The price to enter this ecological paradise makes the experience even better, being completely free, this is a must-go place for anyone around the area. Indoor Activities

Museum Heroes of Chalate

Located within el Manzano Ecological Park, this small museum tells the story of the conflicts that took place in the 1980's in the area. The museum is full of pictures and documents that were important during these conflicts. Equipment that was used, such as bombs, weapons, survival equipment, and more are also featured in this museum.(Page maker) Image: 500 pound bomb that forms part of the museum. Source: Taken by self.




Museum of History and Culture, Dulce Nombre de Maria

Located within the city, this museum has objects of historic value since November 29, 2009 that you could appreciate for only 50 cents. In this museum, you will find different expositions of pieces that belonged to different times. These antiques have been donated by inhabitants of this beautiful city. You will find carpenter tools, minery lamps, sewing machines, and many other artifacts that used to form part of daily life of former habitants of the town. Monsenor Oscar Arnulfo Romero University

The universtity is a project that was born thanks to the vision of a group of professional, who after making a survey of the Department of Chalatenango, came to the conclusion that the necessity for higher level education such as a university was needed. In 1193 the project finally begins. Initially functioning in a local for rent to later have a construction with the right space with 10 acres of land. This university focuses on the formation of professionals with scientific, technical, and human knowledge. In favor of rural development through investigation, teaching, and social projection.
